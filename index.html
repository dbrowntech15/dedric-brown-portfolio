About Me

Building Systems That Actually Work

I’m a Salesforce Product Manager with a deep specialization in Go-To-Market (GTM) systems—the infrastructure that sits at the heart of how modern enterprise sales organizations operate, scale, and win. My career has been defined by one recurring mission: walking into environments where complexity has calcified into friction, and engineering a way out.

Most recently at Cloudflare, I’ve led the evolution of the Salesforce Sales Cloud ecosystem that powers one of the world’s fastest-growing security companies. From architecting an enterprise growth engine that influenced $150M+ in pipeline to integrating NetSuite ERP to collapse a 3-day month-end close to under one day, my work has consistently focused on removing the invisible drag that slows high-performing sales teams down. I also led the evaluation and deployment of Fullcast for territory and capacity planning, enabling 900+ sales reps to operate with clarity and speed during annual planning cycles.

Before Cloudflare, I spent nearly three years at Salesforce itself—first on the Identity & Security team, where I managed a platform protecting $25M+ in regulated contracts and remediating 10+ critical vulnerabilities, and then on the Customer Platforms team where I drove cross-functional product strategy across enterprise CRM initiatives. Earlier in my career at NCR Corporation, I built the Salesforce product management foundation that informs everything I do today—learning how to translate business complexity into platform capability at scale.

Today, I’m most energized by the intersection of AI, automation, and GTM strategy. My work implementing Salesforce Agentforce for GTM sales teams has demonstrated what’s possible when you pair thoughtful product design with emerging AI capabilities—reducing manual data entry by 40%+ and achieving 85%+ prediction accuracy on opportunity outcomes. I believe the next generation of enterprise software won’t just automate tasks—it will amplify human judgment. That’s the future I’m building toward.
